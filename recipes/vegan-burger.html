Matt Pritchard's Dirty Vegan Burger

Description

I went down to stay with my sister for a few days about a year ago, and saw that she had Matt Pritchard's cookbook Dirty Vegan. I decided to make the dirty vegan burger from the cookbook for everyone, but the kitchen was an absolute bombsite once I was finished. My sister's anger at the mess I'd made was so worth it for the taste of these beautiful burgers!

I thought it'd be nice to add a recipe from a proper Welshie like Matt. My favourite Welsh cook is actually Gaz Oakley, who does a traditional Welsh cawl that I haven't made yet. But anyway, this little vegan recipe page wouldn't be right without a proper burger!

Ingredients

  • vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion
  • 30g garlic cloves
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 100g cauliflower, cut into medium pieces
  • 1 Scotch Bonnet chilli
  • 2 x 400g cans of chickpeas, drained
  • 3 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 25g coriander, roughly chopped
  • pinch of salt
  • pinch of cracked black pepper
  • juice of 1 lime
  • 150g oats

To serve

  • 4 bread rolls, split
  • hot chilli sauce
  • mango chutney
  • crisp lettuce leaves
  • vegan tzatziki
  • vegan aioli
  • 4 fat slices of beef tomato
  • red onion, sliced into 12 thin rings

Steps

  1. Fry the onion, garlic and dried spices over a medium heat for a few minutes.
  2. Add the cauliflower pieces and fry for 5 more minutes.
  3. Add the mixture to a food processor with the chilli and combine until finely chopped.
  4. Remove the mixture and blend the chickpeas until smooth.
  5. Add the chickpeas and the mustard to the mixture.
  6. Mix in the coriander, salt, pepper and lime juice.
  7. Slowly add the oats until the mixture begins to stiffen.
  8. Divide the mixture into 4 portions and shape each into a patty.
  9. Fry the patties in oil until golden brown.
  10. Add the toppings to the buns, serve and enjoy!
